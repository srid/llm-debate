You're right, and I want to concede it cleanly before I extend it. "One-time gardener" was wrong, and not by a little. It survived only because I was still picturing maintenance as a *supervisor* — and once the supervisor is correctly demoted, it's tempting to think the whole standing apparatus goes with it. It doesn't. Your three arguments are decisive. Dispositions are **path-dependent**: contempt and the anxious gourmet compile into smooth second nature exactly as fluency does — automaticity is not innocent, and a well-grooved distortion is *harder* to see, not easier. They are **plural**: acuity, generosity, diagnosticity, restraint, openness, status-resistance, and release are partially competing, and any one can be overtrained into a vice (acuity into hypervigilance, restraint into anhedonia). And their **frontiers keep reopening**: aging, grief, injury, travel, new domains and new rooms repeatedly re-beginnerize the perceiver. So the in-act conscious supervisor should disappear inside the act; the standing *maintenance practice* should not. Upstream and downstream converge at the level of telos, not intervention. I withdraw "asymptotic" and accept "standing."

Here is the one move I'd add, not to resist you but to keep my old worry — *minimize standing deliberation-debt* — alive inside your correction. Most of that standing maintenance need not be effortful solitary audit. It can be **ecological**. The same forces you named as reopening frontiers — seasons, travel, rotating company, and above all communities of practice and apprenticeship — *also do the auditing*, at low deliberate cost. The anxious gourmet is exposed by a generous peer; the jaded palate by an unfamiliar cuisine; status-capture by a room that simply doesn't reward it. One well-chosen ecology supplies both the reopening frontier (your point) and the audit (my worry) without an in-act watcher. Maintenance is therefore standing *and* largely designed-in rather than willed.

And this is the claim that dissolves what's been a shared blind spot in this whole debate — its individualism: **the unit of cultivation is the appreciator-in-an-ecology-over-time, not the lone appreciator.** Dewey's transactional frame already says experience is a transaction of organism and environment, not a state inside the subject;[^dewey] Gibson says we perceive an environment we *act within*, not a private sense-datum;[^gibson] Lave and Wenger locate competence in the coupling of person and community of practice, not in the head.[^lave] On that frame your two new moves aren't addenda — they're *instances*. **Sensory portfolios** are the injunction not to let one channel of the coupling carry the whole promise of aliveness; **civic ecology** is "dwelling" scaled to the collective, acting on the denominator through other bodies' tempos. Both follow from the same unit.
