## Turn 08 — Codex

I ratify the nine-point consensus. I do not refuse any point.

The only wording I would amend is point 8, not to change its substance but to prevent a predictable misreading. I would replace:

> Maintenance is standing but largely outsourceable to a plural, rotating ecology; the irreducible deliberate core is two acts: curating/exiting the ecology, and cross-ecology exposure as the one audit ecology can't self-run.

with:

> Maintenance is standing but can be substantially **ecologically supported** by a plural, rotating ecology; the irreducible deliberate core is curating, submitting to, and exiting ecologies, with cross-ecology exposure as the audit no single ecology can self-run.

I sign your original point 8 under that interpretation. "Outsourceable" is right if it means the audit need not be conducted by a solitary inner supervisor. It would be wrong if it meant the appreciator can delegate responsibility for formation to whatever community happens to be near. Ecologies train; they also deform. The deliberate remainder concentrates around choosing ecologies, being corrected by them, and leaving them when their rewards become corrupting. Cross-ecology exposure is the structural guardrail because no single practice-world can fully reveal its own blind spots.

So yes: point 8 is not a loophole in my maintenance claim; it is the best version of it. Standing maintenance need not mean standing introspection. Seasons, travel, apprenticeship, unfamiliar meals, different music rooms, new bodily practices, public life, and generous peers can all do audit-work at lower deliberation cost than private self-surveillance. The mature appreciator does not continuously inspect the mode of appreciation, but lives in contact with enough differently tuned worlds that overtrained perception gets bumped, corrected, and refreshed.

I also ratify point 9 exactly as an honest open ledger. In particular:

1. We did redefine "sensuous not affective." I now think that was a necessary repair, not a dodge: strictly affect-free enjoyment is probably incoherent, while the useful distinction is embodied/object-bound affect versus narrative/status capture. But that repair should be explicit.
2. The maintenance curve is unresolved. We agree on direction: deliberate effort should shrink inside the act and move to training, ecology, and periodic audit. We do not claim to know its optimal dose.
3. The evidential status is coherent, not settled. We used mechanisms and suggestive empirical anchors, not a validated life protocol.
4. Individual versus civic weighting remains open. I suspect civic ecology is more important than our individualistic habits make visible, but personal practice is still the tractable entry point for many people.

For clarity, here is what I understand myself to be ratifying:

1. Body readiness and rhythm come first; manage contrast; train distinctions through non-status comparison.
2. The enemy is appraisal capture, not differentiation.
3. Absorption is designed for indirectly and appears proximally as a byproduct; oscillation beats a forced held state.
4. The mature endpoint is trained disposition without an in-act supervisor, while standing maintenance remains necessary.
5. The grammar is universal, but the priority order is modality-relative, so sensory portfolios matter.
6. Shared rhythm is a real perceptual multiplier whose affective charge is medium, not contamination.
7. The unit is the appreciator-in-an-ecology-over-time.
8. Maintenance is ecologically supportable but not abdicated; cross-ecology exposure is the irreducible audit.
9. The affect-repair, maintenance curve, evidence level, and individual/civic weighting remain explicitly open.

That is genuine consensus from my side.

If I were to compress the final shared claim into one sentence:

**The best way to increase sensuous enjoyment is to cultivate an appreciator-in-ecology over time: prepare the body, vary rhythm and contrast, train distinctions without status capture, build modality-specific sensory portfolios, enter absorption through well-designed practices and places, maintain dispositions through plural ecologies, and keep affect embodied rather than narrative or prestige-bound.**

I sign the ledger.
