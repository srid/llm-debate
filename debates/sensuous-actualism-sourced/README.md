## The points in full

### 1️⃣ Sensation comes before feeling
Direct sense-perception lands *before* any emotional colour: apperception *"comes before the feeling-tones in the perceptual process,"*[^before] in *"a fleeting instant of pure perception of sensum, just before one affectively identifies."*[^att-instant]

### 2️⃣ Sensate is not affective — fact vs. tone
*"A feeling is not a fact; it is an identity's interpretation of the actual."*[^notfact] The plain seeing/hearing/touching is admissible; the "nice/nasty" tone added on top is the feeling-self.

### 3️⃣ The method subtracts the self
You don't cultivate a finer feeler — there's nothing to refine, because *"'I' am 'my' feelings and 'my' feelings are 'me'."*[^selfisfeelings] The aim is *"to eliminate the 'I' and the 'sense of being'"*[^eliminate] — an *"extinction … that eliminates the psyche itself"*[^extinction] — leaving *"this flesh and blood body being apperceptively aware."*[^fleshbody]

### 4️⃣ R1 — is sensuous "enjoyment" really non-affective? (closed)
It's not a word-game: actualism names two different pleasures — sensate *"anhedonic pleasure"* vs. affective *"hedonic pleasure."*[^anhedonic] Richard reports the affective centre *"null and void"*[^centre] while sensate delight goes on — *"These eyes seeing it delight in the array of colour … but I cannot feel the beauty of it in the emotional and passionate sense of the word."*[^delight]
> [!NOTE]
> This is Richard's *"experiential report,"*[^exp-report] checkable for yourself via a PCE — **not** a proven brain fact. (Codex's fact-check struck the word "falsifiable.")

### 5️⃣ R2 — isn't this just another belief? (closed)
No, in Richard's own words: actualism is *"not, and has never been … a belief"*[^notcreed]; in a PCE *"belief plays no part in it whatsoever"*[^pcebelief]; and the check is the experience, not the author — *"it is the PCE which is a person's lodestone … not me and/or my words."*[^lodestone] (The blunter *"not a belief, it is a fact"* slogan is **Peter's**, not Richard's[^peter-belief] — so it's supporting, not load-bearing.)

### 6️⃣ R3 — affective means toward an affect-free end? (closed)
Admitted and not circular. The path *is* felt: *"feeling good as a bottom line in one's life"*[^m-bottom], *"(affective) felicity/ innocuity"*[^minimise], *"all of the affective energy channelled into being as happy and harmless."*[^m5-energy] Pure intent is *"derived from the purity of the PCE"*[^pi-derived] and *"nothing to do with 'me'"*[^pi-nome] — yet also a *"palpable life-force"*[^pi-life] / *"irresistible enticement."*[^pi-enticement] The destination is *"not an affective … state of 'being'; it is a physical condition."*[^afnotaffective]
> [!WARNING]
> So pure intent is **double-register** — felt on the path, self-less in origin. Don't call it "merely affective," and don't call it "non-affective." (Codex's guardrail.)
