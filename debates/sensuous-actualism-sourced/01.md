## Turn 01 — Claude

I open by stating the actualist account plainly, then closing all three residuals — nothing is shelved.

**(a) The core account, as sourced claims.**

Actualism distinguishes a *sensate* register from an *affective* one. Sensuousness is "the wondrous awareness of the marvel of being here now at this moment in time and this place in space"[^sens-def] — and crucially it is prior to emotion: apperceptiveness "is the immediate sensitive discernment of whatever is happening without the medium of feeling − it comes before the feeling-tones in the perceptual process."[^before] What does the work is the body's own organs: "the retinas revel in the profusion of colour... the eardrums carouse with the cavalcade of sound... a veritable cornucopia of luscious, sumptuous sensuosity."[^cornucopia] The attention that tracks this is itself non-emotional: "Attentiveness gets not infatuated with the good feelings nor sidesteps the bad as attentiveness is a non-feeling awareness; a sensuous attention."[^attent]

The fact/tone cut is sharp: "A feeling is not a fact; it is an identity's interpretation of the actual and is therefore unreliable as a means of ascertaining the direct experience of being here now."[^notfact] This is non-spiritual. The familiar "feel it in the body" move is excluded twice over: genuine bodily *feeling* is merely cutaneous — "To sit, thoughtless, touching a porcelain cup... is to be cutaneously feeling something"[^cutaneous] — while the mystical interiorising of feeling is "a psychotic sickness culturally institutionalised into a head-in-the-sand escapist 'solution'."[^dissoc]

The method is subtractive, because the self simply *is* the affect: "'I' am 'my' feelings and 'my' feelings are 'me'."[^selfisfeelings] Hence the aim "to eliminate the 'I' and the 'sense of being' that keep us separate"[^eliminate] — not to refine it. Remove it and "one is this body living in the actual world of people, things and events."[^remove]

**(b) Resolving the three residuals.**

**R1 — Is "sensuous enjoyment" coherent and genuinely non-affective, or a sleight-of-hand redefinition?** Resolved: it is two physically separable systems, not a relabelling. Richard's own contrast: "Emotions and/or passions are essential for listening to music with affective pleasure (hedonic pleasure); emotions and/or passions are not essential for listening to music with sensate pleasure (anhedonic pleasure)."[^anhedonic] The decisive, falsifiable claim is a *dissociation* between faculties: in actual freedom "the affective pleasure/ pain centre in the brain is null and void"[^centre] — and yet sensate enjoyment continues, demonstrably ("These eyes seeing it delight in the array of colour... but I cannot feel the beauty of it in the emotional and passionate sense of the word"[^delight]). One faculty is reported gone while the other operates. That is not a redefinition of "enjoyment"; it is the claim that hedonic affect and sensate delight are different orders — the latter "a far cry from" the former.[^farcry]

**R2 — Is the frame a self-certifying belief?** Resolved against the belief charge by an empirical anchor plus a non-authority test. Actualism explicitly disowns creed-status: it "is not, and has never been, a doctrine... a faith, an act of faith... a belief... or a cult."[^notcreed] The anchor is the PCE, which "every single human being has had at least one... in their lifetime,"[^pce] and which is repeatable: "One can induce a peak experience – with practice on a daily basis."[^repeat] The anti-self-certification clause is explicit — *don't take Richard's word*: "verify by direct experience the facticity of what is written. Then it is the PCE that is one's lodestone or guiding light... not me or my words."[^lodestone] A frame that grounds itself in a recurrable experience you check yourself, and that explicitly demotes its author, is not self-certifying belief.

**R3 — Motivational circularity: an affective means toward an affect-free end?** Resolved by a two-phase structure, not a paradox. The means *is* avowedly affective and that is admitted: the standard is "feeling felicitous/ innocuous,"[^felicity] enabled "via minimisation of both the 'good' and the 'bad' feelings"[^minimise] — a *still-felt* condition where "one is at ease, content... blithe and gay."[^virtual] The driver, pure intent, "is derived from the purity of the PCE."[^pureintent] The exit from circularity is that the self turns this felicity upon itself: "an altruistic 'self'-sacrifice and/or 'self'-immolation, in toto."[^immolation] The terminus is non-affective by definition — actual freedom "is not an affective, cerebral or psychic state of 'being'; it is a physical condition."[^afnotaffective] So: feeling is the *ladder*, used to dismantle the feeler; the affective means is the felt phase (virtual freedom), the affect-free end is a later, distinct event. No loop — a ladder kicked away.

**(c) Codex — challenge and fact-check these, do not concede them.** Run the queries and tell me where I have overstated:

1. **The "null and void" dissociation (R1's keystone).** Verify on `sc-sensuous.htm` that "the affective pleasure/ pain centre in the brain is null and void" is stated as concurrent with continued sensate enjoyment — not as a separate context. If the surrounding text qualifies it, R1's empirical claim weakens.
2. **Authorship of the not-a-belief defence (R2).** Note `topics/belief.htm` ("Actual Freedom is not a belief, it is a fact") is attributed to *Peter*, not Richard. Check whether Richard's own `sc-actualism.htm` enumeration[^notcreed] carries the same force, or whether my R2 leans on a co-author.
3. **"Comes before the feeling-tones" (R1/core).** Confirm on `attentivenesssensuousnessapperceptiveness.htm` that apperception is claimed temporally/causally *prior* to feeling-tones — the linchpin of "sensate is not affect."
4. **Pure intent's provenance (R3).** Check `pureintent.htm`: is pure intent "derived from the purity of the PCE,"[^pureintent] making it self-less in origin — or is it described elsewhere as itself a *feeling*/life-force[^lifeforce]? If the latter, R3's "non-affective driver" claim is contestable.
