## The compressed claim

> **Under actualism, the best way to increase sensuous-not-affective enjoyment is to thin and end the affective "self" so the senses register the actual world directly.** Sensuousness is *sensate (anhedonic) pleasure*[^anhedonic] that comes *before the feeling-tones*[^before]; the method uses HAIETMOBA — *"How is one experiencing this moment of being alive?"*[^haietmoba] — together with feeling-good/felicity[^minimise] and PCE-derived pure intent[^pi-derived] (a felt "life-force"[^pi-life] on the path) to dismantle the feeler; and the end is actual freedom as *"a physical condition,"* **not** an affective state[^afnotaffective] — a self-consuming ladder, affective in means, affect-free at the end.

## The ratified eight points

1. **Sensuousness is pre-affective.** Apperceptiveness *"comes before the feeling-tones in the perceptual process"*[^before]; when one first becomes aware of something *"there is a fleeting instant of pure perception of sensum, just before one affectively identifies."*[^att-instant] Sensuousness itself is *"the wondrous awareness of the marvel of being here now."*[^sens-def]

2. **Sensate ≠ affective — the fact/tone cut.** *"A feeling is not a fact; it is an identity's interpretation of the actual."*[^notfact] Genuine bodily *feeling* is merely *"cutaneously feeling something"*[^cutaneous]; the mystical "feel it in the body" interiorising is rejected as *"a psychotic sickness … escapist."*[^dissoc] The senses' own delight is sensate: *"the retinas revel in the profusion of colour … a veritable cornucopia of luscious, sumptuous sensuosity."*[^cornucopia]

3. **The method is subtractive — the self *is* the affect.** *"'I' am 'my' feelings and 'my' feelings are 'me'."*[^selfisfeelings] The aim is *"to eliminate the 'I' and the 'sense of being'"*[^eliminate] — an *"extinction … that eliminates the psyche itself"*[^extinction] — leaving *"this flesh and blood body being apperceptively aware."*[^fleshbody]

4. **R1 — closed.** Sensuous enjoyment is a category actualism names itself, not our redefinition: *"sensate pleasure (anhedonic pleasure)"* as against affective *"hedonic pleasure."*[^anhedonic] In actual freedom *"the affective pleasure/ pain centre in the brain is null and void"*[^centre] while sensate delight persists — *"These eyes seeing it delight in the array of colour … but I cannot feel the beauty of it in the emotional and passionate sense of the word."*[^delight] This is a *sourced experiential report* — *"an experiential report, written as it is happening"*[^exp-report] — checkable by direct experience (the PCE), **not** proven physiology.

5. **R2 — closed.** Not a belief, in Richard's own terms: actualism is *"not, and has never been … a belief"*[^notcreed]; in a PCE *"belief plays no part in it whatsoever"*[^pcebelief]; and *"it is the PCE which is a person's lodestone … not me and/or my words."*[^lodestone] (Peter's slogan *"Actual Freedom is not a belief, it is a fact"*[^peter-belief] is supporting, not load-bearing.) Closes as **PCE-verification, not creed.**

6. **R3 — closed.** The path is avowedly affective: *"feeling good as a bottom line in one's life"*[^m-bottom], *"(affective) felicity/ innocuity … via minimisation of both the 'good' and the 'bad' feelings"*[^minimise], with *"all of the affective energy channelled into being as happy and harmless."*[^m5-energy] Pure intent is *"derived from the purity of the PCE"*[^pi-derived] and *"has nothing to do with 'me'"*[^pi-nome] — **yet** *"a palpable life-force"*[^pi-life] *"experienced as an irresistible enticement."*[^pi-enticement] The end is *"not an affective … state of 'being'; it is a physical condition."*[^afnotaffective] So: **a self-consuming ladder — affective in means, affect-free at its end — not non-affective all the way down.**

7. **Guardrail — pure intent.** It must be stated in the site's *double register*: PCE-derived and *"nothing to do with 'me'"*[^pi-nome], yet experienced as a *"palpable life-force"*[^pi-life] / *"irresistible enticement"*[^pi-enticement]. Neither "merely affective" nor "non-affective."

8. **Guardrail — wording.** Quote Richard's own *"comes before the feeling-tones"*[^before]; do not translate the dissociation into neuroscience or call it "falsifiable."
