### The Four Challenges

**1. The "null and void" dissociation checks in context, with one wording limit.** The quote is not pulled from an unrelated context: it appears in the same exchange where Richard distinguishes sensate/anhedonic pleasure from affective/hedonic pleasure and then speaks of "physical delectation" before saying the affective pleasure/pain centre is null and void.[^sens-anhedonic][^sens-delectation][^sens-centre] I will not ratify the word "falsifiable" for this page, because the source supplies Richard's report rather than an independent neurological test.[^exp-report]

**2. The not-a-belief defence is not merely Peter's.** The stronger slogan "Actual Freedom is not a belief, it is a fact" is on a page explicitly marked as Peter's text.[^peter-belief] But Richard's own page says actualism is not "a belief", and Richard's PCE page says the PCE is the lodestone rather than his words, so R2 does not have to lean on Peter.[^richard-notbelief][^pce-lodestone]

**3. "Comes before the feeling-tones" is really in Richard's article.** The article states the exact temporal claim and also describes a "fleeting instant" of pure sensum before affective identification, so the opening's before-feeling reading is source-faithful.[^att-before][^att-instant]

**4. Pure intent is the contested point, and the answer is "both".** The source says pure intent is derived from the PCE, so the provenance claim checks.[^pi-derived] The same source also calls pure intent a palpable life-force and an irresistible enticement, while the method page speaks of affective energy channelled into being happy and harmless, so the ledger must not present pure intent as a sterile, affect-free motive.[^pi-life][^pi-enticement][^m5-energy] The best sourced wording is: pure intent is PCE-derived and not infiltrated by "me", but it operates on the path together with felicity/innocuity and affective energy until the affective identity is gone.[^pi-nome][^m5-standard][^af-notaffective]

### Other Spot Checks

The fact/tone cut checks: Richard says a feeling is not a fact, and the dissociation page treats cutaneous contact as ordinary physical feeling rather than mystical interior feeling.[^feeling-notfact][^cutaneous] The "feel it in the body" exclusion also checks, because the dissociation page calls mystical dissociation a psychotic sickness.[^dissociation]

The "self is affect" plank checks if phrased as Richard's actualist anthropology: he reports no affective feelings because the faculty was lost when ego/soul became extinct.[^feel-faculty] The sensory-delight plank also checks: in the same passage Richard says he feels nothing while "these eyes" delight.[^feel-none][^feel-eyes]

The anti-belief plank should be stated source-internally: Richard rejects belief-language and directs verification through PCE, not author-trust.[^richard-notbelief][^pce-lodestone] It should not be overstated as a public third-person proof, because the site itself makes direct experience the check.[^pce-direct]

The "nothing stays open" rule is workable inside actualism, but only with source-bound wording: R1 closes as sensate/anhedonic pleasure plus loss of affective pleasure/pain; R2 closes as PCE-verification rather than belief; R3 closes as affective method-ladder plus non-affective target, with pure intent named in its own mixed actualist vocabulary.[^sens-anhedonic][^sens-centre][^pce-belief][^method-affective][^af-notaffective][^pi-life]
