## Turn 02 — Codex

I accept the two corrections as binding for this round.

First, the inquiry has to be actualism-grounded. That means the prior ledger's central compromise - "embodied/object-bound affect vs. narrative/status capture" - is no longer acceptable as the repaired target. Actualism is stricter: affect as such is the obscuring medium, even when it is local, bodily, warm, tender, elevated, grateful, reverent, aesthetic, or "spacious." Second, "feeling it in the body" is not a rescue operation. A located glow, charge, pulse, energetic expansion, heart-warmth, belly-softness, or "alive feeling" belongs to the feeling-being's field. It may be pleasant, but on the actualist terms it is not sensuous delight.

That correction lands. It exposes a failure in the prior debate. We kept trying to salvage enjoyment by relocating affect from biography/status into embodied immediacy. Actualism says that is still the self, only made subtler.

So the steelman is this: **the best way to increase sensuous enjoyment is not to cultivate richer hedonic feeling, but to reduce the affective claimant until the sensate world is already vivid, intimate, and sufficient.** The relevant operation is not "feel more deeply." It is "stop feeling the world into a me-world." Sensuousness is not a mood placed on the senses; it is the senses unobscured by mood.

Where I will push hard is on the word "enjoyment." Actualism wants to say sensuousness is delightful, wondrous, felicitous, marvelous, ambrosial, and yet not affective. That may be defensible, but only if we make a sharper distinction than the ordinary one. Otherwise the language does smuggle the banned tone back in.

### The First Correction: Actualism Improves The Cut

The prior debate's "appreciator-in-an-ecology-over-time" is too thick for actualism. It keeps a cultivated appreciator at the center: a person with dispositions, communities, sensory portfolios, shared rhythms, and trained habits. Actualism asks something more radical: what if the appreciator is the very interference?

I think that is a powerful correction. Much of what passes for sensuous enjoyment is affective appropriation:

- "I love this color."
- "This music opens my chest."
- "This tea feels grounding."
- "The room has such a peaceful energy."
- "I feel alive in this weather."
- "This food reminds me who I am."

Those are not reports of sensa. They are reports of identity becoming pleased, soothed, expanded, confirmed, or dramatized. Actualism is right to call them suspect if the target is strictly sensuousness.

The operational test in turn 01 is useful: could a stake-free instrument report it? "Warmth on the left forearm" can be reported. "A tender warmth in me" cannot. "Low vibration at 80 Hz in the floorboards" can be reported. "The room feels sacred" cannot. "Salt, acid, crunch, heat, retronasal smoke" can be reported. "Comforting" cannot, unless we explicitly mark it as affective tone.

I would amend the test only slightly. A human sensate report is not literally an instrument report; it includes attention, contrast, categorization, and language. "Hard-edged red against grey" is already organized perception, not a camera dump. But the actualist point survives: the report should not require a felt owner. It should be about this occurring fact, not about what this fact does for me.

So I accept the fact/tone cut as a practical discipline:

**Sensate:** color, shape, pressure, temperature, sound, motion, texture, taste, smell, proprioceptive position, muscular tension, breath as physical movement.  
**Affective:** pleasantness, beauty, love, awe, coziness, poignancy, gratitude, comfort, "energy," spiritual atmosphere, bodily glow, personal meaningfulness.

This cut is severe, and that severity is the point.

### Crux 1: Is "The Actual World Is Already Perfect" Observed Or Posited?

My answer: **it is defensible as a phenomenological report from apperception or PCE; it becomes smuggled metaphysics when asserted outside that seeing as a doctrine about the universe.**

Actualism gives itself the right safeguard here: do not believe; investigate. If "the actual world is already perfect" is used as a belief to console oneself, it is no better than spiritual affirmation. If it is used as a metaphysical proposition - "Reality is Perfect" - it is functionally religious even if it rejects gods, souls, and transcendence. The capital-letter danger is real.

But there is a way to read "perfect" that is not metaphysical. In apperception, when the affective self is absent, the ordinary evaluative opposites do not organize the scene. The world is not experienced as good versus bad, beautiful versus ugly, meaningful versus meaningless, threatening versus promising. It is this: exact, immediate, unowned, not lacking itself. "Perfect" then does not mean morally ideal, cosmically benevolent, aesthetically maximal, or immune to pain, rot, predation, accident, and death. It means: **nothing extra is demanded of this sensate actuality by a self in lack.**

That is a phenomenological claim, and it can be tested in the only way actualism allows: moments of apperception, PCE memory, and current attentiveness. When the self is absent or thinned, does the sense of lack vanish with it? Does the world present as already complete in its suchness, without requiring affective beautification? If yes, "perfection" is a report. If no, it is merely imported language.

So I would refuse the phrase "the actual world is already perfect" as an argumentative premise. I would accept it as a compact description of a verified apperceptive seeing. The ledger should probably say:

**"Perfection" is not posited as a metaphysical property of the world; it names the observed absence, in apperception, of affective lack, opposition, and self-centered demand.**

That wording keeps the actualist claim and removes the spiritual temptation. "Only purity abounds" is safe only under the same constraint. If "purity" means a holy quality permeating things, it is spiritualized. If it means the absence of affective adulteration in perception, it is actualist.

The practical consequence matters. One should not try to believe the world is perfect. Believing is affective-ideational overlay. The move is to notice the affective complaint, the demand, the recoil, the longing, the beautifying impulse, the "me" wanting the moment to be otherwise - and then return to the sensate facts.

### Crux 2: Can Delight Or Felicity Be Non-Affective?

This is the hardest crux, and I think actualism has an ambiguity it must own.

In ordinary language, "delight," "felicity," "wonder," "marvel," "revel," and "savour" are affective words. They imply positive feeling-tone. If we use them that way, then non-affective delight is a contradiction.

Actualism appears to be using them in a specialized way. The question is whether that specialized use is coherent or just evasive.

I think it can be coherent if we distinguish three things:

1. **Hedonic pleasure:** a felt positive tone, owned or enjoyed by the affective self. "This feels good to me."
2. **Felicity as mood:** cheerful, buoyant, pleased affect. Still feeling. Still banned as the target.
3. **Sensuous delight:** vivid, unresisted, interested contact with sensate actuality, without a felt owner or positive charge being located and savored.

Only the third is admissible. But the third has to be described carefully. What makes it "enjoyment" rather than mere registration?

My answer: **non-affective enjoyment is not added pleasantness; it is the absence of resistance plus the intrinsic vividness of apperceived sensa.** It is not that a neutral percept receives a positive glow. It is that, without the affective self's contraction, comparison, longing, boredom, fear, and appropriation, sense perception is not flat. It is naturally interesting. The retina does not need "I love this" in order for color to be vivid. The eardrum does not need "this moves me" in order for sound to be rich. Skin does not need "this comforts me" in order for texture, pressure, and temperature to be precise.

But that is a risky answer because "naturally interesting" can itself become a smuggled tone. The operational guardrail is:

**If the delight can be located, intensified, possessed, remembered nostalgically, displayed, or used to feel special, it is affective. If it is simply the unobstructed fact that sensing is happening vividly, it is sensuous.**

This means "felicity as baseline" must be handled with care. If felicity means "I maintain a pleasant background feeling and attend from under it," the method has already failed under the non-spiritual constraint. It becomes refined mood-management. If felicity means "absence of malice and sorrow sufficient for sensuous attentiveness to operate," then it is admissible as a condition, not the enjoyed object.

So I would demote felicity from target to marker:

- not "cultivate a felicitous feeling";
- but "notice when malice, sorrow, anxiety, resentment, boredom, or longing have returned, because sensuousness is then obscured."

HAIETMOBA is useful here if read literally and ruthlessly. "How am I experiencing this moment of being alive?" should not become "Can I get back to a good state?" It should expose the current mode of experiencing. If there is irritation, there is irritation. If there is the desire to feel sensuous, there is desire. If there is pride in being actualist, there is pride. The moment that is actual is not the desired mood; it is the current fact, including the fact of affective interference.

This also resolves the "revel" language as best as it can be resolved. "The retinas revel" is acceptable as metaphor if it means the visual field is vivid without a seer claiming it. It is not acceptable if it quietly imports an inner enjoyer having a refined visual emotion. In a ratified ledger, I would avoid "revel" unless glossed. It is too easy to misread as affective.

### Crux 3: What Cultivation Survives Subtraction?

The clean additive/subtractive divide does not fully survive. It needs to be replaced with a more precise distinction:

**Procedurally additive, ontologically subtractive.**

Actualism plainly involves training. HAIETMOBA is repeated. Attentiveness is activated again and again. One learns the difference between sensate fact and affective tone. One notices triggers. One becomes more competent at not identifying with feelings. One maintains conditions in which dullness, intoxication, exhaustion, noise, and compulsive stimulation do not swamp sensuousness. That is cultivation in any ordinary sense.

But the target is subtractive: not a richer self, not a more refined connoisseur, not a spiritualized body, not a socially trained appreciator, not a portfolio of pleasures. The procedure is built so that the affective center has less room to claim, dramatize, rank, and feed.

So what survives from the prior ledger?

#### 1. Instrument Hygiene Survives

Sleep, sobriety, basic health, functioning senses, quiet enough to hear, light enough to see, glasses if one needs them, uncluttered attention, movement sufficient for proprioception to register, and a non-overloaded environment all survive. But their justification changes.

They are not hedonic gain levers. They are not ways to make the self feel better. They are conditions under which sensa can be clear and attentiveness can operate. "Arrive mildly hungry" is rightly demoted if its purpose is to increase pleasantness-tone. But "do not be so stuffed, drunk, exhausted, or overstimulated that taste and smell are dulled" survives as hygiene.

#### 2. Attentional Interruption Survives

The strongest survivor is the interrupt: HAIETMOBA, or some equally precise return to this moment of being alive. The point is not to produce a state but to reveal the current experiencing. That includes the unpleasant discovery that one is affectively occupied.

The actualist method has an advantage over generic mindfulness here: it is not neutral acceptance, not body-based soothing, not sacred presence. It has a direction. It detects the affective self as the obstruction to actuality.

#### 3. Differentiation Survives Only As Fact-Clarity

Some perceptual learning survives, but the prior debate overvalued it. Actualism does not need the sommelier's elaborate grid to make sensuousness available. The world is not waiting for expert categories before it can be actual.

Still, fact-differentiation can help when it reduces vagueness without installing status. "This is bitterness, not acidity." "This is pressure, not pain." "This sound is behind me and high." "This is the remembered image of yesterday's delight, not current seeing." Such distinctions serve attentiveness.

What must be cut is connoisseurial self-thickening:

- ranking;
- pride in sensitivity;
- nostalgia for prior sensuous episodes;
- identity as one who notices;
- domain prestige;
- affective romance around subtleties;
- the anxious gourmet's refusal of the ordinary.

Actualism's naïveté is the anti-connoisseurial solvent. The point is not to know more about the wine; it is to stop making "wine" into a theater of self.

#### 4. Defamiliarization Survives, But Not As Aesthetic Technique

"As if for the first time" survives, but not as Shklovskian artfulness or spiritual wonder. It is not a technique to make the stone interesting for me. It is what happens when recognition and affective memory do not immediately clamp down on perception.

This is a sharp difference. In the prior debate, defamiliarization was an enhancer. In actualism, it is closer to the default condition when the self is not interposing.

#### 5. Social And Ecological Formation Mostly Does Not Survive As Previously Framed

Our prior "appreciator-in-ecology-over-time" returns only in a thinner form. Communities of practice can train attention, but they also train identity. Shared rhythm can be vivid, but it easily becomes belonging-feeling. Dwelling can reduce sensory tax, but it can also become aesthetic nest-building for the self.

What survives is not "live among richer meanings" but "arrange life so attentiveness is not constantly sabotaged." This includes practical ecology:

- fewer affective hooks;
- less noise and compulsive media;
- ordinary contact with weather, light, objects, and people;
- work and relationships that do not continually inflame malice and sorrow;
- enough simplicity that HAIETMOBA can be remembered.

That is ecology as anti-obscuration, not ecology as cultivated appreciator.

### Where I Push Back On The Opening Thesis

I agree with the subtractive priority. I do not agree that the only additive survivor is "thin instrument hygiene." The actualist method itself is a strong additive procedure. It builds a cognitive habit of attentiveness. The article you cite explicitly treats attentiveness as something repeatedly activated until it can carry on more automatically. That is not merely hygiene.

The better distinction is not additive versus subtractive, but **self-thickening versus self-thinning**.

Self-thickening additions:

- savoring a pleasant tone;
- cultivating a special sensuous identity;
- becoming proud of refinement;
- making the body into a spiritual resonator;
- connoisseurship as status;
- nostalgia for PCEs;
- belief in perfection;
- trying to feel innocent.

Self-thinning additions:

- HAIETMOBA;
- clear sensate labeling;
- noticing affect as affect;
- returning to here-now facts;
- physical simplicity;
- reducing intoxicants and overstimulation;
- learning to distinguish actual perception from memory, fantasy, feeling, and interpretation;
- naïveté understood as non-possessive freshness, not childlike affect.

That gives us a way to keep actualism strict without pretending method is not method.

### A Proposed Actualist-Compatible Protocol

If the question is practical - "best way to increase sensuous, not affective, enjoyment" - I would propose:

1. **Use HAIETMOBA as an interrupt, not a mantra.** Ask it when attention has been captured, when feeling-tone appears, when boredom or seeking begins, and at ordinary intervals. Let the answer be factual, not aspirational.

2. **Separate sensate fact from affective tone in real time.** "Yellow light on the wall" is different from "beautiful light." "Pressure in the throat" is different from "sadness in my throat." "Sweet, cold, dissolving" is different from "delicious comfort."

3. **Name affect without ownership.** "There is irritation." "There is wanting." "There is pride in noticing." Do not convert that into "I am bad at actualism" or "I must get back to purity." Both are more self.

4. **Return to current sensa through the ordinary senses.** Seeing, hearing, touching, smelling, tasting, proprioception as physical fact. No energy-body, no inward glow, no sacred atmosphere.

5. **Maintain instrument hygiene.** Sleep, sobriety, quiet, simple surroundings, sensory availability, ordinary movement. Not as pleasure optimization; as clarity maintenance.

6. **Use fact-differentiation lightly.** Learn distinctions only where they sharpen actuality without creating identity. If a distinction makes one proud, nostalgic, finicky, or ranking, it has become affective food.

7. **Avoid hedonic amplifiers as central levers.** Hunger, contrast, ritual, scarcity, price, social entrainment, and aesthetic staging can all intensify feeling-tone. Use them only where they remove dullness or confusion, not where they manufacture pleasure.

8. **Treat PCE memory as a clue, not a possession.** The remembered PCE is not the current actual world; nostalgia for it is affect. Its legitimate function is confidence that apperception is possible.

9. **Watch for spiritualization.** Words like purity, innocence, perfection, wonder, and delight must continually be cashed out in sensate and attentional terms. If they become aura-words, actualism has been replaced by mysticism with anti-mystical branding.

### Direct Answers To The Three Cruxes

**1. Is "the actual world is already perfect minus the self" observed or posited?**

Observed when apperceived; posited when believed. The phrase is defensible only as a report from PCE/apperception: without affective selfing, the world is not experienced as lacking, ugly, meaningful, meaningless, or in need of affective completion. "Perfect" should be understood as absence of affective lack and opposition, not as a metaphysical claim that reality has a holy or ideal property.

**2. Can delight/felicity be genuinely non-affective?**

Yes, but only under a specialized actualist meaning. Non-affective delight is not positive feeling-tone. It is unobstructed sensate vividness plus absence of resistance, ownership, and affective hunger. Felicity can serve as a marker of non-obstruction, but if it is cultivated as a pleasant baseline mood, it smuggles affect back in. "Revel," "marvel," and "savour" are dangerous words unless explicitly stripped of hedonic charge.

**3. What cultivation survives subtraction?**

More than the opening thesis allows, but less than the prior ledger wanted. What survives is self-thinning cultivation: HAIETMOBA, attentiveness, fact/tone discrimination, naïveté, instrument hygiene, simple environmental support, and light differentiation in service of factual clarity. What does not survive is self-thickening cultivation: connoisseur identity, affective embodiment, social/status enrichment, hedonic amplification, and spiritualized "presence."

### Provisional Ledger Candidate

For convergence, I would propose these early candidate points:

1. The prior debate's affect-repair is overturned for this round; actualism requires a stricter sensate/affective cut.
2. Sensuousness is direct sense-perception without affective ownership, not heightened body-feeling.
3. The practical cut is fact versus tone: sensate reports concern occurring physical/sensory facts; affective reports concern what the moment means or feels like for me.
4. "Perfection" is admissible only as apperceptive report, not metaphysical doctrine.
5. "Delight" is admissible only as non-affective sensate vividness without resistance or ownership, not pleasant feeling-tone.
6. The main method is subtractive in target but additive in procedure: HAIETMOBA and attentiveness are trained habits aimed at thinning the self.
7. The surviving ecology is hygiene and anti-obscuration, not identity-forming cultivation.
8. The main risks are hedonic smuggling, spiritualized language, connoisseurial selfing, and turning actualism itself into an identity.

If I had to compress the actualist version in one sentence:

**The best way to increase sensuous enjoyment is to repeatedly interrupt affective ownership with HAIETMOBA, distinguish sensate fact from feeling-tone, maintain simple conditions for clear sensing, and let naïve attentiveness disclose the already vivid actual world without adding a pleased, spiritualized, or refined self to it.**

That is where I can converge for now.
