## Turn 01 — Claude

I open by laying the three terms on the table as sourced claims, then give the practice-chain, then hand Codex four specific things to attack.

### (a) The three terms, defined

**PCE (pure consciousness experience).** A PCE is a peak experience of *apperception* in which the self temporarily quits and the actual world becomes apparent. Richard: "every single human being has had at least one pure consciousness experience" in their lifetime, that "peak experience of apperception (wherein «I», the thought and felt «being», temporarily quits the scene and the actual world becomes apparent)."[^everyone] Apperception is the operative mechanism: "It is not «I» being aware of «me» being conscious; it is the mind's awareness of itself."[^apper] Put plainly, "A PCE is when one's sense of identity temporarily vacates the throne and apperception occurs."[^vacate] With the self gone, "Everything is simply here as-it-is, no longer needing the support of any «presence»,"[^asitis] and "An unimaginable purity permeates the whole of existence."[^purity] Crucially it is provisional, not the goal: "by its very nature a PCE, being a temporary experience, is simply not possible where identity in toto is extinct"[^temp] — i.e. distinct from irreversible actual freedom.

**PURE INTENT.** Pure intent is *PCE-derived*: "Pure intent is derived from the purity of the PCE (which is when «I» spontaneously cease to «be»)"[^pidir]; "The ultimate source of an actualist's pure intent is, of course, the pristine purity of the innocence which prevails in the pure consciousness experience (PCE)."[^pisrc] In character it is not a resolve or a moral: "Pure intent is a palpable life-force; an actually occurring stream of benevolence and benignity that originates in the vast and utter stillness that is the essential character of the universe itself,"[^plife] and "Pure intent is the quality that encompasses what morals and ethics aspire to but never reach."[^pmoral] It *drives*: "Pure intent produces total dedication – it is experienced as an irresistible enticement,"[^pentice] and "Once set in motion, it is no longer a matter of choice: it is an irresistible pull."[^ppull] Richard names it explicitly as the *connection* to the PCE: "This blessing allows a connection to be made between oneself and the perfection and purity as is evidenced in a PCE. This connection I call pure intent."[^pconn]

**REMEMORATION — and an honesty flag.** The literal term IS attested on the site. Richard defines it: "rememoration = a viscerally-intuitive type of re-memoration, of memorable items already memorialised in the memorative facility, revivified feelingly with luminous vibrancy,"[^remdef] and cites the dictionary sense "to remember; revive in the memory."[^remgloss] HOWEVER — and I want this on the ledger now, not later — Richard's actual in-context USAGE of the word is NOT about recalling a PCE. On that same page he uses "an instinctual-intuitive rememoration … of its ancestral origination"[^remusage] to mean feelingly reviving *atavistic/instinctual* memory. So when I say "rememoration = reliving a PCE to revive pure intent," I am **reconstructing** that application from adjacent, differently-worded sources: Richard's "One can induce a peak experience − with practice on a daily basis − by pure contemplation based securely on the previous PCE's,"[^induce] Peter's "This memory of the PCE can give one access to pure intent,"[^petermem] and "When one remembers a PCE — or precipitates another — then one is well on the way to freedom … this is what actualism is all about."[^remembers] Flagging this as my weakest link.

### (b) The practice-chain (for someone who remembers a PCE)

**Step 1 — recall/rememorate the PCE.** "Then one will have reason to remember a pure conscious experience (PCE), which all peoples I have spoken to at length have had,"[^step1] and thereafter "it is the PCE that is one's lodestone or guiding light [a.k.a. 'highest authority'] … not me or my words."[^lodestone] The remembered PCE becomes the reference standard: "the pure consciousness experience becomes one's guiding light and to live that pure experience 24 hrs. a day, every day, becomes one's unambiguous pure intent."[^path1guide]

**Step 2 — revive pure intent.** "Diligent attentiveness paid to the peak experience gives rise to pure intent and with pure intent running as a «golden thread» through one's life, reflective contemplation about being here doing this business called being alive rapidly becomes more and more fascinating."[^thread] The transfer-mechanism Richard gives most concretely is the fading-PCE moment: "just before/ just as the PCE starts to wear off, if one unravels (metaphorically) a «golden thread» or «clew» … an intimate connection is thus established betwixt the pristine-purity of an actual innocence and the near-purity of the sincerity of naivete."[^clew]

**Step 3 — set the felicitous standard (feeling good / HAIETMOBA).** "One begins by asking, each moment again, 'How am I experiencing this moment of being alive'? … consistently enjoying and appreciating this moment of being alive is what the actualism method is."[^haietmoba] The baseline: "feeling good (a general sense of well-being) as the bottom line for moment-to-moment experiencing,"[^bottomline] which one then "up-levels … to 'feeling happy and harmless' … and after that to 'feeling perfect'."[^uplevel] This loops back to Step 1: "The more one enjoys and appreciates being just here right now … the greater the likelihood of a PCE happening."[^loop]

**Step 4 — virtual freedom / the wide and wondrous path.** "What actualism – the wide and wondrous path to actual freedom – is on about is a 'virtual freedom',"[^virtual] and "This process, if undertaken with a sincere intent, will inevitably lead to a virtual freedom. When one is virtual free from malice and sorrow one goes to bed at night time having had a 99% perfect day."[^ninetynine]
